Moana - Educational Webpage

Introduction

Moana is a Disney movie that captures the adventurous journey of a young polynesian girl as she sets out to save her people. This webpage provides a educational overview of the movie, it's characters, and it's themes.

Story Overview

Moana, the daughter of the chief Tui, and sail to find Maui, a demigod to help her return the heart of Te Fiti. Moana and Maui ecounter monsters and other challenges on their voyage across the pacific and Moana learns the power of faith and persistence.

Main Characters

Moana

Moana is courageous, determined, and headstrong. She manages to save her family, restore the balance of nature, and council a Demigod out of his self-loathing and back into his own heroic tale.

Maui

Maui is a demigodn trickster and shapeshifter and is distinguishable by his bulky tattooed body, thick, flowing hair and magical fish hook that allows him to shift into vaious creatures.

Grandma Tala

Tala is the mother of Chief Tui and grandmother of Moana. Because of her passion for ancient Polynesian myths and legends, Tala is regarded as the "village crazy lady".

Additional Insights

Plot

The plot highlights themes of courage, identity, and the importance of preserving one's culture. Moana's journey is both physical and emotional as she grows into a true leader

Setting

The story is based on Polynesian culture and folklore.
The title Moana means "oceans" in many Polynesian languages.
The island of Motunui is inspired by real-life islands like Fiji, Samoa, and Tonga.
